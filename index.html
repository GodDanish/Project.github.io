Dal Bhat Tarkari

Dal Bhat Tarkari is a popular dish in Nepal. It consists of rice (Bhat), lentils (Dal), and a variety of vegetables (Tarkari). It is a staple dish in Nepalese cuisine and is eaten at least twice daily in almost every Nepali household

Dhido

Dhido (ढिँडो) is a traditional Nepalese porridge prepared by cooking ground millet or cornmeal flour. It is traditionally cooked in a taapke (cast-iron pan) and is mostly consumed in the mountainous regions where wheat and rice are difficult to grow. The porridge is often paired with pickles, yogurt, curried vegetables, or homemade butter. It is recommended to consume it as soon as possible since it hardens as it cools.
